Happy Hour Tower isn’t just a game. It’s a social phenomenon.


Informal gatherings often get off to a slow start, right? Guests standing around with others they know. Newcomers off to the side, limiting eye contact. Not with Happy Hour Tower. I’ve created a fun way to “break the ice”, “get the party started” and put everyone in a great mood!

Soon after the game begins, you’ll notice people making new friends, competing with old ones, laughing, cheering and sometimes sighing! By the end of the game, everyone is mingling and talking about what a good time they had!

And Happy Hour Tower is not only for home use. Imagine it at work in the lunch & break areas, conference room or at a company function. Use its team building powers to bring employees together as they rely on each other’s creativity, dexterity and competitiveness to assemble the winning masterpiece.

How To Play Happy Hour Tower

Object of the Game
To build a tower using different sized geometric pieces of synthetic ice without it collapsing.

Game Parts
48 geometric ice pieces of varying sizes including rectangles and square blocks, round rods, triangles, and rings. The 3 larger rectangle ice blocks are to be used as the base for the tower.

The Set-Up
The game is for players of all ages. You can play individually or create teams. Place up to 3 base rectangle blocks on any flat surface ensuring accessibility from all angles and scatter the ice pieces around the game. These base pieces must touch each other.

Start of Play
Begin by choosing the starting player using whatever criteria you like e.g. shortest, tallest, oldest, youngest, etc.) and specify the order of play. e.g. clockwise, etc. Every player must remember who they follow. Each player chooses an ice piece and places it on the top of a base block or another piece. The turn moves to the next person or team member.

Rules
  • Place your ice piece anywhere on the base structure or on any existing ice piece;
  • If an ice piece is shifted, the play continues provided none of the pieces fall;
  • Your turn is complete 1) after the ice piece is placed on the tower without any pieces falling 2) tower remains stable for a minimum of 5 seconds;
  • If any part of the tower collapses or an existing ice piece falls, the player responsible is out of the game. Any fallen pieces not still on the base are removed from the area and put back into play; the game continues with the next player;
  • If the tower collapses on its own 1) after the 5 second minimum or 2) due to wind, a table bump, etc.) the game continues with fallen pieces put back into play.

Winning The Game
The last player to successfully place an ice piece on the tower wins the game. If all of the pieces have been placed, then all remaining players win the game.

Variations
  • Use a timer if you like and set it as desired to add a dimension of urgency.
  • Assembly made be made more difficult by removing one of the three bases.
  • At the start of the game, divide the pieces of ice evenly amongst all players or teams.
  • For team play, consider blindfolding a partner, relying on verbal commands only to guide the placement of ice pieces.
  • A die may be used to determine which piece of ice a player must use. i.e.
